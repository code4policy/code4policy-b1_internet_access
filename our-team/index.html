Naman is a master of education student at Harvard University. As an engineer turned educationist, he works at the intersection of technology and education. His previous leadership projects include leading the Solar Digital Night Schools at Barefoot College International, co-founding Vidyalaya Vistaar/ School Plus and founding Palak. As an entrepreneur, Naman is a recipient of numerous awards including the Harappa Habit Hero award in 2020 for ensuring education continuation for children during Covid19, the 2019 Karmaveer Chakra Awards in association with the United Nations, and the 2019 Rashtriya Gaurav Samman by the National Youth Awardees Federation of India. He is also a founding member participant at Anubhav Lecture Series, an initiative of Harris School of Public Policy, UChicago. His work in child education have been covered across various digital and print media including a leading front page cover story by The Dainik Bhaskar (highest circulated daily newspaper in India), identifying Naman as a leading youth leader involved in nation-building.

Abdurrehman is currently a Master in Public Policy Candidate at the Harvard Kennedy School. He has worked in Strategy and Operations roles with governments in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Oman and Pakistan. His areas of interest include energy, infrastructure, transport and State-Owned Enterprise reform. He now wants to leverage insights from data and behaviour sciences to improve developmental outcomes in emerging economies.

As a Master in Public Administration in International Development student at Harvard Kennedy School, Siew aims to work in the intersection of technology and policy by using data analytics to address pressing development issues. During her 5 years of work experience with Axiata, a telecommunications conglomerate, Siew complete various rotations across Axiata South Asia and Southeast Asia operating companies, focusing on using analytics in audit,finance, and fraud prevention. Most recently, as the Strategy Consultant of Axiata Digital Capital (ADC), their digital micro-financing venture serving underserved nano, micro, small and medium sized enterprises, Siew led a funding exercise and supported application of ADC for a digital banking license. Using technology for social justice, Siew has also developed a chatbot for migrant workers that serves as an information channel, data collection tool and multilingual reporting system for trafficking and forced labor incidents.

Rohini is a John F. Kennedy fellow pursuing the Master in Public Policy (MPP) program at Harvard Kennedy School. She is a Statistics graduate and comes with over 4 years of policy research and development experience in the Indian public/non-profit space. Most recently, Rohini worked for J-PAL South Asia where she helped design a research study to reduce drug consumption among youngsters, through qualitative interviews, stakeholder engagement, and data analysis. Prior to J-PAL, she led implementation and monitoring for a large-scale randomized control trial (RCT) to study female labor force participation at Evidence for Policy Design. Rohini has also served as a part-time facilitato for STEM subjects for low-income public school students in India. Rohini is data enthusiast and passionate about gender, labor, and education policies.
