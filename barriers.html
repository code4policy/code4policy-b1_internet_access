A multiplicity of factors impede mobile internet adoption by women in the global South

This chart displays the percentage of mobile users who identified the following factors as the most important barrier to using mobile internet. The selected countries have the largest gender gaps in mobile internet access.

Cost of mobile phones, literacy (reading/writing difficulties), and lack of understanding about how to use mobile internet stand out as the three primary barriers to mobile internet access and usage among women in most of the developing countries listed above. This also indicates that while women might be increasingly owning and using mobile phones, phones that can enable internet access i.e. smartphones, still remain unavailable to a large section of women across the globe. Literacy levels of women also seem to be impacting their ability to use internet on their mobile phones, despite being aware that such facilities exist.
